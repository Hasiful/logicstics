Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  • Registration Services are needed because most countries demand that imported products conform to national and international standards. Products, particularly in food and beverage, pharmaceuticals and cosmetics need to be tested before they can be sold. Tradeling has a partnership in place with SGS to offer customers a lean and seamless Product Registration experience. We help ensure items are appropriately registered and meet the standards set by relevant local and regional authorities.
    This services gives you the certainty that products are approved for distribution and use in the MENA region.
  • In order to import or export you need a licence. In cases where you don't have an export or import licence, Tradeling can act as the Importer of Record, or Exporter of Record, giving you the agility and flexibility to trade internatiionally.
  • An export licence is a document issued by the relevant authority, for instance, the Chinese government, that gives exporters in China permission to ship goods out of the country.
    If the exporter doesn’t have an export licence, the goods will not be cleared through Chinese customs when being exported.
  • An export licence is a way for a country to control the goods going out of the country – just like an import license is a way to control incoming goods.
    The import or the export can be restricted or prohibited to meet the demands of the domestic market and consumers and protect the national economy.
  • The responsibility for obtaining an export licence is on the company whose goods are being exported. For example if a Chinese company wants to export goods from China to overseas markets, they are responsible for obtaining the export licence, either by applying for the license themselves or by letting a trading agent handle it.
  • The company that wants to import goods from an overseas market into a country are responsible for obtaining the import licence from the relevant authority in that country, either by applying for the license themselves or by letting a trading agent handle it.
  • If you do not have an import license, Tradeling offers an IOR (Importer of Record) service through its trusted service providers. This allows you to import the goods.
  • Today, most exporters have export licences. However, some don’t.
    These exporters are often smaller companies that want to avoid the bureaucracy and the cost that comes with applying for the export licence.
  • If your supplier lacks an export licence, Tradeling offers an EOR (Exporter of Record) service through its trusted service providers. This allows your supplier to export the goods.
  • Yes, most products are regulated. For a more detailed explanation of regulated products, see Tradeling Inspection Services. Tradeling offers support with product registration and quality certifications of all types. Our logistics experts will guide you through the process. In partnership with SGS Services, we provide goods inspection and quality control. For more information, please contact our Logistics team.
  • The responsibility for insuring your cargo is determined by the Incoterm used in your shipping contract. For instance, under the FOB Incoterm, the seller's responsibility ends when a carrier takes possession of them, or, with respect to ocean shipments, when the merchandise is placed safely aboard the vessel or when an on board Bill of Lading has been issued. The buyer is responsible for insuring the goods from that point onwards.
